Jollof Rice Recipe

Description

This is a personal recipe on how I cook my Jollof rice. ^_^

NOTE:: I'm not adding measurements

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Tomato paste
  • Curry Powder
  • Dried Thyme
  • Onions
  • Bell pepper
  • Fresh Tomatoes
  • Scotch bonnet
  • Salt
  • Stock cubes (Knorr chicken)
  • Groundnut oil

Steps

  1. Heat up water, this can be in a pot or a kettle
  2. Chop up an onion
  3. Wash the fresh tomatoes, scotch bonnet and bell pepper
  4. Put the tomatoes, scotch bonnet, bell pepper and an onion into a blender and blend
  5. After blending pour into a bowl
  6. By now the water should be boiling already, put your rice into a bowl and add the boiling water, steer the rice then cover it with a lid (this could be flat plate or the lid of a pot
  7. Place an empty pot on the cooker, add a bit of vegetable oil
  8. Add the chopped onion into the pot, and let if fry for a little, pay attention to it so it doesn't burn
  9. Add the tomatoe paste next and steer, allow to fry for about two mins
  10. Pour the blended tomatoes and pepper into the pot
  11. Add salt, stock cubes, curry and thyme
  12. Add stock (gotten after boiling your chicken, checkout fried chicken recipe Click here)
  13. Add about half cup of water, then allow to boil for about 3-5 mins, while you wait for that proceed to the next step
  14. Drain the water from the rice (use a sieve)
  15. Add the rice to the boiling stew,(by now it should be about 3-5mins already)
  16. Mix the rice with your spoon/spatula, This should be the only time you mix the rice still it is fully cooked
  17. Add just enough water to cover the rice
  18. Leave to cook, check regularly, when you notice the water's dried up, use your spoon to take a bit of rice, taste to find out if it's soft enough
  19. If no add a bit more water and repeat the previous step, if yes move on to the next step
  20. Wait till the water dries up do not panic because it's burning, this is normaal
  21. When there's no more water, steer the rice and be mindful of the burnt part, then your Jollof rice is ready to be served
